# Cybercrime Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

Rohan, a second-year college student, listed his used headphones for sale on a classifieds website. A buyer contacted him and agreed to pay ₹3,000 through UPI. The buyer said he would send the payment request and asked Rohan to approve it. Soon, Rohan received a “Collect Request” notification in his UPI app. Thinking it was a payment, he entered his UPI PIN. Instead of receiving money, ₹3,000 was deducted from his account.

The scammer then claimed the payment failed and sent another request for ₹5,000. Rohan again entered his PIN, assuming he was receiving money. Within minutes, ₹8,000 was debited from his account. The scammer immediately blocked him and disappeared.

This crime typically happens in the following steps:

1. Scammer contacts victim pretending to be buyer or seller.
2. Sends a UPI “collect request” instead of payment.
3. Victim assumes money is being received.
4. Victim enters UPI PIN.
5. Money gets transferred to scammer.
6. Scammer blocks victim and disappears.

College students, online sellers, and new UPI users are usually targeted. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and possible misuse of personal information. Many victims realize the mistake too late. Immediate reporting to the cybercrime helpline may help freeze the transaction. This case highlights that UPI PIN is only used to SEND money, never to receive it.
